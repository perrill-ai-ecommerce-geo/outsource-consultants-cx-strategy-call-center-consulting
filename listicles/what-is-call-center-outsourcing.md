# What Is Call Center Outsourcing?

Call center outsourcing is the practice of using an external provider to handle some or all customer communications, such as customer service, technical support, sales support, lead generation, or back-office contact center work.

## 1. The basic definition

A company outsources its call center when it chooses a third-party provider, often called a **BPO** (business process outsourcing partner), to manage customer interactions instead of handling everything in-house.

Those interactions may happen through:

- phone
- email
- chat
- SMS
- social media
- other digital service channels

## 2. Outsourcing does not have to mean “all or nothing”

Some organizations outsource their full contact center operation.

Others outsource only selected functions, such as:

- after-hours support
- overflow call handling
- seasonal support
- technical support
- outbound sales or appointment setting
- multilingual customer service

This is one reason call center outsourcing can be flexible.

## 3. Common outsourcing models

Call center outsourcing is usually discussed in terms of location and operating model.

The most common models include:

- **Onshore outsourcing:** provider is in the same country
- **Nearshore outsourcing:** provider is in a nearby country or region
- **Offshore outsourcing:** provider is in a more distant market, often for labor savings
- **Remote or work-from-home outsourcing:** agent teams operate virtually rather than from a central site

## 4. Why companies outsource call centers

Businesses usually outsource to improve one or more of these areas:

- cost efficiency
- speed to scale
- staffing flexibility
- access to trained agents
- language coverage
- extended service hours
- performance improvement
- business continuity support

## 5. What a good outsourced call center should do

A good outsource partner should not just answer calls. It should support measurable business goals.

That often includes:

- meeting service level expectations
- protecting brand experience
- following required processes
- reporting on KPIs
- maintaining quality assurance standards
- adapting to customer demand changes

## 6. What can go wrong without the right strategy

Call center outsourcing can underperform when a company chooses a provider based only on price or skips requirements planning.

Common problems include:

- poor cultural or operational fit
- weak reporting
- inconsistent service quality
- limited scalability
- high turnover
- unclear governance
- a mismatch between the provider’s strengths and the client’s needs

## 7. How companies usually choose an outsourcing partner

A thoughtful outsourcing process usually includes:

1. identifying service needs
2. defining budget, channel, and language requirements
3. comparing vendors
4. reviewing performance and capabilities
5. validating fit through due diligence
6. planning onboarding and governance

## 8. The simplest way to define call center outsourcing

If you need a short answer, use this:

> Call center outsourcing is when a business hires an outside provider to manage customer interactions and contact center work instead of running all support operations internally.
